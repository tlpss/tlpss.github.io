Thomas Lips

Phd Student in Robotic Manipulation

Hi There,

I’m Thomas, a first year PhD Student at the AI and Robotics Lab at Ghent University, . My research interests center around Robot Manipulation, with the ultimate goal of creating robot systems that can deal with the entropy of the real world and do not require cages, so that they can assist humans in everyday life. In our lab, we focus on learning manipulation skills for deformable and articulated objects. Some topics that I find particulary interesting (and that I am hence focusing on) are spatially structured representations (think keypoints), sim-to-real, reward learning from human demonstrations and combining multiple sensory modalities (vision + touch) for robot learning.

Before I embarked on this PhD journey, I obtained my bachelor and master (European system) in Computer Science Engineering with the highest distinction at Ghent University. During my masters I also studied at EPFL, where I completely fell in love with Lausanne.

Next to being very passionate about robotics and technology, I’m a.o. interested in hiking, (Macro-)Economics, Politics, sports, Philosophy of Science and thinkering.

Feel free to reach out if you want to talk, I am always eager to learn.
