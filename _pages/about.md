Hi There, 
I'm Thomas, a first year PhD Student at the [AI and Robotics Lab](https://airo.ugent.be/) at [Ghent University](https://www.ugent.be/en) :belgium:. My research interests lie in the area of Robot Manipulation, with the ultimate goal of creating robot systems can deal with the entropy of the real world and do not require cages. At our lab, we focus on manipulating deformable and articulated objects. Some avenues that I find particulary promising (and that I am hence focusing on) are spatially structured representations (think keypoints), sim-to-real, reward learning from human demonstrations and multimodal (vision + touch) robot learning. 

Before I embarked on this PhD journey, I obtained my bachelor and master (European system) in Computer Science Engineering with the highest distinction at Ghent University. During my masters I also studied at EPFL, where I completely fell in love with Lausanne.

Next to being very passionate about robotics and technology, I a also like in a.o. hiking, (Macro-)Economics, Politics, Sports, Philosophy of Science and thinkering.

Feel free to reach out if you want to talk about anything related to (the path towards) general-purpose robotic manipulation, I am always eager to learn.
